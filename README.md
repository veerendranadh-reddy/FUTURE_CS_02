## 🎯 Objective
The objective of this task is to analyze phishing emails, identify suspicious indicators, and raise awareness about phishing attacks.

---

## 📩 Sample Email Analysis

### Email Example 1

Sender: support@paypa1.com  
Subject: Urgent: Verify Your PayPal Account Immediately

Indicators of Phishing:
- The sender domain is misspelled (paypa1 instead of paypal)
- Urgent language used to create panic
- Suspicious login link

Classification: **Phishing**

---

### Email Example 2

Sender: security@bank-alert.com  
Subject: Account Security Alert

Indicators:
- Generic greeting (Dear Customer)
- Request to click on a link to verify account details
- Suspicious external link

Classification: **Suspicious**

---

### Email Example 3

Sender: noreply@github.com  
Subject: New login from a new device

Indicators:
- Legitimate domain
- No suspicious links
- Informational message only

Classification: **Safe**

---

## ⚠️ Common Phishing Indicators

- Fake or misspelled email domains
- Urgent or threatening language
- Suspicious links
- Requests for personal information
- Unexpected attachments

---

## 🛡 Prevention Tips

- Always verify the sender's email address
- Avoid clicking unknown links
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Report suspicious emails
- Use email spam filters

---

## ✅ Conclusion

Phishing attacks are one of the most common cyber threats. By identifying phishing indicators and educating users, organizations can reduce the risk of credential theft and financial fraud.
